# Perceptual Mapping Blueprint

## Safety

### Step 1: Dimension-Specific Qualitative Archetypes
- **Typical High**: Clean streets, presence of streetlights, maintained buildings, visible sidewalks.
- **Typical Low**: Less lighting, dilapidated structures, narrow or absent sidewalks, graffiti.
- **Ambiguous**: Mixed elements, well-lit but aged structures, unclear visibility due to obstructions.

**Visual Switches**:
- Lighting conditions
- Building maintenance
- Sidewalk clarity
- Presence of graffiti or vandalism

### Step 2: Dimension-Specific Quantitative Scaling
- **Semantic Signatures**:
  - Positive correlation: Dim 2
  - Negative correlation: Dim 5
- **Interpretation**: High safety often corresponds to well-maintained and brightly-lit environments as suggested by positive Dim 2 scores.

### Step 3: Precise Boundary Logic & The 'Equal' Threshold
- **Zone of Indifference**: CLIP vectors with low variation in Dim 2 and Dim 5 combined with mixed visual cues.
- **'Equal' Rule**: Use "Equal" if CLIP signal delta between images is < 0.20, and the images lack strong safety indicators like lighting.

## Beautiful

### Step 1: Dimension-Specific Qualitative Archetypes
- **Typical High**: Lush vegetation, aesthetic architecture, clean and organized spaces.
- **Typical Low**: Sparse vegetation, industrial zones, lacking aesthetic appeal.
- **Ambiguous**: Urban areas with some greenery but mixed structural appeal.

**Visual Switches**:
- Vegetation density
- Architectural appeal
- Cleanliness
- Presence of artistic elements

### Step 2: Dimension-Specific Quantitative Scaling
- **Semantic Signatures**:
  - Positive correlation: Dim 4
  - Negative correlation: Dim 7
- **Interpretation**: High scores relate to areas with high vegetation or appealing architecture.

### Step 3: Precise Boundary Logic & The 'Equal' Threshold
- **Zone of Indifference**: Instances where vegetation and architecture are balanced.
- **'Equal' Rule**: Choose "Equal" if CLIP signal delta is < 0.15 with mixed aesthetic and minimal vegetation indicators.
